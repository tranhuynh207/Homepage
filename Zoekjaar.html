SEARCH YEAR VISA IN THE NETHERLANDS

In this topic, I will share about the Search Year Visa (Zoekjaar Visa) for graduates to look for jobs after their graduation. This is all based on my experience and aims to share information that I wished I knew before I started my journey under this Visa. So, what you can find here?

Disclaimer: This is based on my experience applying in 2021, things could be changed at the time you apply, please consult IND's website for the exact information. Useful links are provided in the end of the article

What is Search Year Visa, and how to apply for it?

The Search Year Visa, or Zoekjaar Visa, is a residence permit offered by the Netherlands to international graduates. It allows recent graduates to stay in the country for up to a year to find a job or start a business.

  • Open work permit: No restrictions on employer sponsorship during the visa period.
  • Eligibility: Graduates from recognized Dutch institutions or select foreign universities (check IND's list).

    • Application steps:

  • Gather Documents: Degree certificate, proof of identity, recent passport photo, etc.
  • Submit Application: Apply online via the IND website or by post.
  • Pay the Fee: Ensure your application fee (approximately €200 as of 2021) is paid.
  • Await Approval: Processing times vary, but it typically takes 2–6 weeks.

    • Timeline for the application process: my application

    I applied in end-2021 from abroad (Those apply in the Netherlands will do a little bit different). Here’s my timeline:

  • 01 November 2021: Called the local embassy to book appoitment to apply for zoekjaar visa
  • 29 November 2021: My appointment with the embassy. I came there to get my fingerprint collected. I received a letter from the embassy with my v-number, came home and sent my scanned documents to the address stated in the letter.
  • 29 February 2022: Got my approval. IND informed that the process could take up to 3 months but due to backlogs, it could be even longer
  • March 2022: I came back to the embassy to give my passport, left it there and asked the embassy to send it by post to my home once I got the visa sticker
  • 08 April 2022: I flew to the Netherlands. A new journey started.

    • What should you prepare before your departure to the Netherlands?

  • Legal Documents: Ensure you have originals and certified copies of your diploma, passport, and birth certificate.
  • Health Insurance: Arrange for Dutch health insurance (mandatory once in the Netherlands).
  • Accommodation: Look for temporary housing options before arriving.

    • Website for housing: Kamernet, funda, Facebook (be aware of scams)

    Set up once you are in the Netherlands

  • Municipal Registration (BRP): Register your address with the local municipality to receive your BSN (Citizen Service Number).
  • Bank Account: Open a Dutch bank account for transactions like rent and salary.
  • Health Insurance: Activate your Dutch health insurance plan. For those who haven't had a job yet, consider apply zorgtoeslag to get some help to pay for health insurance
  • Job Search: Update your CV to the Dutch format and start networking.

    • Note: Use platforms like LinkedIn, Indeed.nl, and Glassdoor for job hunting.
